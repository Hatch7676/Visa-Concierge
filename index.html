Real-World Scenarios
1
Dinner Reservations — Any Vibe
Casual, upscale, rooftop, harbor views — tell them exactly what you're in the mood for and they'll find it, check availability, and book it. Great when you just don't want to research.
I'm in Newport Beach tonight, want dinner around 7pm. Quality casual — not fancy — rooftop or harbor views if possible, fresh seafood or American classics, around $25–$40/person including a drink.
2
Full Trip Planning or Itinerary Help
They coordinate hotels, flights, ground transport, tours, and dining — sequenced and booked. Many people hand off entire vacations so they can just show up. Also great for pre-booking restaurants before you land so it's done on arrival.
Flying into Chicago next weekend for 3 days. Help plan a relaxed itinerary: hotel under $300/night downtown, 2–3 great dinner spots with good atmosphere, and tickets to a baseball game or museum. Also book us dinner Friday and Saturday night — casual, good food, nothing too fancy.
3
Ground Transport Coordination
Airport transfers, full-day drivers, multi-stop days — they source vetted options and book it. Far better than piecing together rideshares when you're moving around a lot or have a long day planned.
I need a reliable private driver in Nashville for a full day — roughly 6 hours, 4 stops across the city. Looking for a clean, professional option under $300 for the day. What are my options?
4
Recreation & Local Excursions
Once you're at a destination and want to fill a day — fishing charters, snorkel tours, private guides, cooking classes, hiking, water sports. They find reputable outfitters and book it for you.
I'm in Cancun for 3 days and want to fill tomorrow morning. Looking for a private snorkel or cenote tour for 2, well-reviewed, under $150 each. What's available?
5
Hard-to-Get Event Tickets or Experiences
They have access to presale and harder-to-find tickets for concerts, sports, theater, and unique behind-the-scenes experiences that aren't available through normal channels.
Looking for concert tickets in Austin next month in the $150–$300 range, or a Broadway show in New York in June. Also interested in any behind-the-scenes or VIP experience options if available.
6
Gift Ideas, Purchasing & Delivery
They handle research, purchase, and delivery — no scrambling while you're traveling. They can ship directly to a hotel, home, or recipient address.
My wife's birthday is in two days while I'm on a trip. Can you send a nice flower arrangement and gift basket to our home in Salt Lake City — thoughtful, under $150 total, maybe chocolates or a spa item?
7
Emergency or Unexpected Situations
Canceled flights, weather delays, lost luggage, last-minute hotels — they do the legwork while you're dealing with the chaos. Pay with your CSR and save receipts for reimbursement if the airline offers nothing.
My flight was canceled in Miami due to weather and the airline isn't offering anything. Can you find me a well-reviewed hotel near the airport tonight — nicest option under $350 — and help rebook a morning flight out?
8
Authentic Local Food Experiences
Not tourist traps — actual neighborhood spots locals go to. Better than any search engine because they curate for your vibe, time window, and budget rather than returning 200 generic results.
I have a free afternoon in New Orleans and want to eat where locals actually go — not Bourbon Street. Casual, great food, authentic to the city. Budget around $20–$35/person. What do you recommend and can you get me in?

Meals, lodging, and incidentals
Up to $500 per trip · $10,000 per year maximum
Airline voucher rule
If airline offers nothing — you're covered
A digital offer you never accepted or redeemed is not the same as a voucher used. If the airline covers it, you can't double-claim. If they offer partial coverage, you may claim the gap.
What to do in the moment
1
Call the Concierge first — get the hotel booked and flight rebooked while availability exists.
2
Pay everything with your CSR — hotel, meals, incidentals. Required for coverage.
3
Save every receipt — hotel folio, meals, anything you spend during the delay.
4
Get a delay notice from the airline — email, app notification, or screenshot. Required for the claim.
5
File after you're home — through the Chase benefits portal below.
⚠️
Do not accept a hotel voucher from the airline if you plan to claim through CSR — use the concierge to book your own and keep receipts instead.
